## 🧑‍💻 Who I Am

I'm **OCC** — a software engineer, building cross-platform mobile apps with **Flutter/Dart** and high-performance backend systems with **Rust/Axum**. I ship production products and open-source tooling under my freelance engineering brand **O.C. Code**.

I have a strong bias toward **developer tooling and open-source ecosystems**. Right now I'm building **Nib** — a registry-based Flutter component and animation library modelled after the shadcn/ui model. The flagship product, **NibMotion**, is a Framer Motion equivalent for Flutter that operates at the RenderObject level with real spring physics, declarative exit animations, and reactive motion values.

On the backend side, I reach for **Rust** every time performance and reliability are non-negotiable. I've shipped production Rust/Axum APIs backed by PostgreSQL with Firebase FCM, Dockerized and deployed to Render. I'm increasingly focused on systems-level Rust — custom allocators, `unsafe`, and `no_std` environments.

I also hold a **BSc in Computer Science** and spent time as a Flutter instructor, leading a 16-week course that took students from zero to shipping their first Flutter app on the Play Store.

## 🚀 Featured Projects

<details>
<summary><b>🌀 NibMotion</b> — Framer Motion for Flutter</summary>
<br/>

> A production-grade animation system for Flutter engineered at the **RenderObject level**.

**What makes it different:**
- Spring physics engine with configurable stiffness, damping, and mass — not just curves
- `NibPresence` for declarative exit animations — components unmount *after* they finish animating out
- `MotionValue` reactives — read and react to animated values without triggering full widget rebuilds
- Composable and framework-agnostic — works alongside Riverpod, Bloc, or bare `setState`
- Inspired by Framer Motion's API ergonomics, built ground-up for Dart

**Stack:** Flutter · Dart &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Status:** 🔨 Active development

<br/>
</details>
